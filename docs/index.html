Suite Documentation

Technical specifications and user guides for v1.1.3

1. Stereo Architecture

The suite utilizes a Discrete Stereo Routing engine. Unlike standard audio players, our therapy modules generate independent signal chains for the Left and Right channels. This allows users to compensate for asymmetric hearing loss or ear-specific tinnitus frequencies.

  • Lock L/R: Synchronizes both channels to the "Master" Left ear settings.
  • Master Gain: A global multiplier stage that preserves your custom L/R balance while scaling overall loudness.

2. Mobile Precision Controls

Recognizing the limitations of standard sliders on touchscreens, we have implemented a multi-tier control system:

  • Sliders: For coarse, rapid frequency sweeps.
  • Fine-Tune Buttons (±1, ±5): For exact pitch matching without needing a keyboard.
  • Coarse Step Buttons (±10, ±100): For quick navigation across octaves.

3. Validation & Safety

To ensure clinical integrity, the suite includes automated diagnostic tools:

  • Hardware Phase Audit: Checks for inverted headphone wiring which can negate the effects of decorrelated noise.
  • DSP Validation: A real-time mathematical check of filter attenuation (Target: >40dB) and frequency accuracy (Target: ±1%).
  • Recalibration Cycle: The system highlights the validation tool every 30 days to ensure hardware consistency.

4. Data & Privacy

The Trahreg Suite is a Private Progressive Web App (PWA):

  • Local Persistence: All settings and usage logs are stored in your browser's localStorage.
  • Offline Mode: All signal processing happens locally on your device; no active internet connection is required after the initial load.
  • Installation:
    • Desktop/Android: Click "Install App" in the Tools menu on the dashboard.
    • iOS (Safari): Tap the 'Share' icon and select 'Add to Home Screen'.

5. Therapy Guidelines

General recommendations based on current research:

  • Notch Therapy: 1-2 hours daily. Results typically manifest over 3-6 months.
  • Neuromodulation: 20-30 minute sessions, twice daily.
  • Volume: Always set levels at or slightly below your tinnitus "mixing point."

6. Troubleshooting & Error Resolution

The suite performs real-time validation of the audio signal. If you see a failure in your clinical report or the status bar, follow these steps:

"Shallow Notch" Failure

This error occurs when the DSP engine detects that the notch filter is not reducing sound energy by at least 40dB at your target frequency. Clinical research suggests that a deep notch is required to effectively trigger lateral inhibition.

  • Disable Audio Enhancers: "Volume Boosters" or "Equalizer" browser extensions often compress the audio signal, which "fills in" the notch. Disable these during therapy.
  • System Post-Processing: Turn off "Spatial Sound," "Dolby Atmos," or "Audio Enhancements" in your Windows/macOS sound settings. These algorithms interfere with precision filter slopes.
  • Sample Rate Alignment: Ensure your OS audio output is set to 44.1kHz or 48kHz. Extreme sample rate mismatches can lead to mathematical rounding errors in filter calculations.

"Frequency Mismatch" Failure

Occurs when the measured output frequency deviates by more than 1% from the target. This is typically caused by virtual surround sound software re-sampling the audio in real-time.
