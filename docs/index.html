Overview

This tinnitus therapy suite is an open‑source, browser‑based collection of sound tools designed to support tinnitus relief and sound therapy experimentation. Everything runs locally in your browser using the Web Audio API. No accounts, no tracking, no data collection.

Therapy Modes

1. Decorrelated Noise Therapy

Generates left/right decorrelated broadband noise designed to reduce auditory synchrony.

2. Notch Therapy

Plays broadband noise with a narrow frequency band removed around the user’s tinnitus pitch. Leverages lateral inhibition to potentially reduce tinnitus loudness by suppressing neurons in the specific frequency range associated with the user's symptoms.

3. Lenire‑Style Sound Therapy (Sound‑Only)

An experimental implementation inspired by the auditory component of bimodal neuromodulation research. Utilizes carrier tones, frequency‑shaped noise, amplitude‑modulated noise, and tone‑burst patterns designed to promote neural plasticity.

4. Broadband Sound Therapy

Provides high-fidelity white, pink, and brown noise, alongside nature-inspired soundscapes (rain, ocean, stream, wind) optimized for long-term auditory masking and habituation.

5. Noise Generator (Python‑style)

A specialized tool for creating calibrated 16-bit WAV files for offline use. Supports adjustable notch widths (octaves), variable sample rates, and precise noise coloring algorithms.

Research & Methodology

This suite is built upon core principles found in modern tinnitus research:

  • Bimodal Neuromodulation: Implements the sound-only stimulation patterns used in clinical trials, such as synchronous tone bursts.
  • Auditory Synchrony Reduction: Decorrelated noise patterns are used to disrupt the pathological firing synchronization of neurons often seen in tinnitus patients.
  • Lateral Inhibition: Frequency-notched audio aims to re-balance auditory cortex activity.

Session Tracking & Persistence

The suite includes a local-first persistence layer powered by storage.js:

  • Automatic Logging: Therapy minutes are tracked and stored in a daily usage log.
  • Settings Memory: Your specific frequencies, balance settings, and volume levels are remembered between sessions.
  • Privacy: No data is transmitted. All usage logs and settings stay in your browser's local storage.

Diagnostic & Visualization Tools

Notch Finder

Helps users identify their specific tinnitus frequency using a narrow band noise slider.

Two‑Tone Comparison

Allows A/B comparison of two distinct tones to assist in precise pitch matching.

Frequency Sweep

Plays a linear frequency sweep from low to high to help explore hearing range and sensitivity.

Spectrogram

Real‑time FFT visualization to verify notch placement and analyze hardware frequency response.

Audio Level Meter

Displays peak and RMS amplitude to ensure safe listening levels and prevent audio clipping.

Offline Portability

The entire suite is designed to function without an internet connection:

  • Local Access: Open index.html directly from your file system.
  • Mobile Use: On iOS/Android, use "Add to Home Screen" to save the tools as a local app.
  • Offline Files: The Python generator script allows you to create therapy files for use on any standard MP3 player.

Recommended Use

For best results, use:
• Wired earbuds
• Bluetooth earbuds (AirPods, Galaxy Buds, etc.)
• Over‑ear headphones
• USB‑C or Lightning headphones
• Bone‑conduction headphones
• Hearing aids with Bluetooth streaming

Start with low volume and short sessions. Increase gradually based on comfort. Stop immediately if you feel discomfort.

Medical Accuracy Standards

This suite conforms to research-based clinical specifications:

  • Notch Precision: Center frequencies are validated within ±1% of the user target.
  • Attenuation Depth: Notch filters are engineered for a minimum of -40 dB reduction at the center frequency.
  • Decorrelation: Dual-buffer and temporal offset methods (randomized time-shifts) ensure independent neural stimulation in each ear.
  • Loudness Safety: Normalized output levels to prevent clipping and sudden volume spikes during therapy transitions.
