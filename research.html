Tinnitus Research Overview

Evidence behind sound‑based therapies.

Bimodal Neuromodulation (Lenire-style)

Lenire uses sound and tongue-stimulation. This site provides the sound-only component, which serves as a foundational baseline for experimental auditory stimulation. Research indicates pairing auditory stimulation with attention can promote cortical plasticity.

Key Findings

  • Clinical trials report significant reductions in tinnitus severity for many.
  • Benefits are often most pronounced in moderate-to-severe cases.

Sound Therapy Approaches

Sound therapy aims to reduce the contrast between tinnitus and the environment, facilitating habituation.

Common Methods

  • Notch Therapy: Removing noise at the tinnitus frequency to encourage cortical reorganization.
  • Broadband Noise (White/Pink): Useful for sustained masking and reducing attention focus.
  • Decorrelated Noise: Reduces synchrony in the auditory system.
